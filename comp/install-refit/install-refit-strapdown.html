# How to install rEFIt on an external USB drive --- ## Basically [this](http://refit.sourceforge.net/doc/c1s1_install.html) with pretty pictures. ### 1. Download rEFIt from [here](http://prdownloads.sourceforge.net/refit/rEFIt-0.14.dmg?download). ![Alt text](img/1.png) ### 2. Open the disk image. ![Alt text](img/2.png) ### 3. Wait, the disk image isn't needed just yet... ### 4. Plug your flash drive in... ![Alt](img/3.jpg) ![Alt](img/4.jpg) ### 5. Open Terminal Type `diskutil list` and press enter Should like something like this: ![Alt](img/5.png) The USB disks are always at the bottom. You can see mine is listed as: ![Alt](img/6.png) In most cases, the `IDENTIFIER` for your USB flash drive is something else. In my case, it is `disk3` _Make sure to note your correct `IDENTIFIER`, otherwise u gon erase the wrong disk._ ### 6. Erase USB flash drive using Terminal In Terminal, type `diskutil eraseDisk JHFS+ "USB" GPT /dev/disk3` but replace `disk3` with _your_ `IDENTIFIER`. Press enter. POOF there goes your data. It gon look like dis: ![Alt](img/7.png) ### 7. Copy the "efi" folder to the USB drive Recall that our USB flash drive was renamed to `USB` in the erase process. In Terminal, type `cp -R /Volumes/rEFIt/efi /Volumes/USB` and press enter Then, `cd /Volumes/USB/efi/refit/` and press enter Then, `sudo ./enable.sh` and press enter YOUR PASSWORD AIN'T GONNA SHOW but type it in and press enter anyway. Why? Because UNIX. Should look like this: ![Alt](img/9.png) ### Done. Eject the USB flash drive like you normally would, then reboot your Mac while holding the option key, and you should see rEFIt as an option. ![Alt](img/10.jpg) --- Copyright &copy; wheelsandbytes 2015-2017.
